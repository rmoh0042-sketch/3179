The big picture

This section gives the overall trend of Australian domestic tourism in 2025. It shows how trip volume and spending changed across the year before looking deeper into location and travel purpose.

Domestic trips increased across 2025, showing stronger travel activity by the December quarter.

Spending dipped in the middle of the year but recovered to nearly $40.0B by December.

Takeaway: Domestic tourism became stronger toward the end of 2025, with both trips and spending rising by December.

Where Australians travelled

This section shows how domestic tourism was distributed across Australia. The map and ranking charts highlight the states that attracted the most trips and generated the highest spending.

Domestic tourism was concentrated in the eastern states, with NSW, Victoria and Queensland forming the strongest travel cluster.

NSW and Victoria led trip volume, showing that domestic travel demand was strongest in the larger eastern states.

NSW generated the highest travel spend, while Victoria and Queensland also contributed strongly to domestic expenditure.

Takeaway: The eastern states dominated domestic tourism, especially NSW, Victoria and Queensland.

Why Australians travelled

This section explores the main reasons behind domestic travel. It compares holiday, visiting friends and relatives, business, and other travel purposes across trips and visitor nights.

Holiday travel was the main reason Australians travelled in December, followed by visiting friends and relatives.

Holiday travel stayed the dominant purpose across 2025, while business travel remained a smaller share.

Holiday and VFR travel produced the most visitor nights, showing that leisure and family-related trips created longer stays.

Takeaway: Holiday travel was the main driver of domestic tourism, while VFR travel also created many visitor nights.

How Australians travelled

This section looks more closely at travel behaviour. It compares day and overnight trips, shows how travel purposes changed from March to December, and connects trip volume with spending.

Day trips were higher than overnight trips in most states, while ACT was the exception with slightly more overnight trips.

Holiday and VFR travel increased from March to December, while business travel declined across the year.

States with more trips generally generated more spending, with NSW standing out as the strongest travel market.

Takeaway: Most states relied heavily on day trips, while high trip volume was closely linked with higher spending.
