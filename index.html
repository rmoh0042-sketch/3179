The big picture

Domestic travel in 2025 was steady but not flat. Trip numbers gradually increased across the year, while spending dipped mid-year before rising again in December.

Trips rose from 94.1M in March to 100.3M in December.

Spending recovered to nearly $40.0B by the December quarter.

NSW and Victoria recorded the highest domestic trip volumes, while Queensland also remained a major travel destination.

Holiday travel was the largest purpose category, followed by visiting friends and relatives.

Holiday travel stayed the largest purpose category across 2025, while business travel formed a much smaller share.

Holiday and visiting friends or relatives(VFR) produced the highest number of visitor nights.

New South Wales, Victoria, and Queensland generated the highest domestic travel expenditure.

New South Wales, Victoria, and Queensland recorded the highest domestic trip volumes.

Day trips were higher than overnight trips in every state, especially in NSW and Victoria.

Holiday and VFR travel increased from March to December, while business travel declined.

NSW combined the highest trip volume with the highest domestic spend, while Queensland showed strong spend relative to its trip count.

Data: Tourism Research Australia, Domestic Tourism Statistics, December quarter 2025.
